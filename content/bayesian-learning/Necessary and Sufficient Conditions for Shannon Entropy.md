>[!definition] Necessary & Sufficient Conditions
>- [[= Shannon entropy is maximized at the Uniform Distribution]]
>- [[= Entropy is unchanged by probability-zero outcomes]]
>- $H(p)$ is continuous w.r.t. all arguments
>- [[= Chain Rule for Entropy]]



## Discussion


>[!example] Entropy for Two States
>Suppose $\Theta=\{ \theta_{1},\theta_{2} \}$. The entropy of any belief $(q,1-q)$ is,
>$$
>H(q)=-q\ln(q)-(1-q)\ln(1-q)
>$$

In this general form for entropy of two states, the **necessary** and **sufficient** conditions, up to rescaling, are that $H(p)$ be [[= Shannon entropy is maximized at the Uniform Distribution|maximized at the uniform distribution]], [[= Entropy is unchanged by probability-zero outcomes|unchanged by probability zero outcomes]], _continuous_, and satisfy [[= Chain Rule for Entropy]]. That is, properties 1-4. 

In the curve depicted below, it is _concave_, minimized at the two degenerate distributions $(0,1)$ and $(1,0)$, and maximized at the uniform distribution $\left( \frac{1}{2},\ \frac{1}{2} \right)$.
