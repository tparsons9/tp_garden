**Prior-dependent cost functions** are models where the cost of acquiring a signal depends on the agent's initial (prior) belief; the _cost of absorbing_ the information content of a signal varies with what the agent _already knows_. 

These cost functions are typically defined over the domain of the [[Prior Probability|prior beliefs]] and their corresponding [[Bayes' Plausible]] posterior distributions $\mathcal{T}(p)$. We further define, 

$$
\mathcal{S}=\{ (p,\tau): p\in\Delta(\Theta),\  \tau\in \mathcal{T}(p) \},
$$
to be the domain of the prior beliefs and Bayes' plausible distributions.

The cost functions under **prior-dependent costs** take the form $C:S\to \mathbb{R}$. The broad class of such functions are under [[Posterior Separable Cost Functions]], or the sub-class [[Uniformly Posterior Separable Cost Function]].
