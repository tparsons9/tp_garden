The agent's beliefs will [[Asymptotic Agreement of Beliefs|asymptotically converge]] to the parameter, within their subjective model, that is "closest" to the true distribution. Closeness, in this context, is measured by the KL-Divergence, [[= Kullback-Leibler Divergence for Relative Entropy]]. The agent settles on the model parameter that minimizes the KL-divergence from the true distribution.

#### Mechanism

The outcome of [[Model Uncertainty and Misspecification under Bayesian Learning#Model Misspecification|misspecified learning]] can be understood heuristically by examining how the agent updates their beliefs over two potential parameter values, $\theta \in \{ A,B \}$, where the prior probability of $\theta=A$ is $\pi \in(0,1)$.

Use $f_{\theta}(x)$ to denote the conditional probability of signal realization $x$ when the parameter is $\theta$. The agent observes a sequence of signals drawn i.i.d. according to $f_{\theta ^{\ast}}$, the distribution generated by the true parameter $\theta ^{\ast}$ (which the agent may not consider possible, i.e., $\theta ^{\ast}\not\in \{ A,B \}$).

For any sequence $\mathbf{x}_{t}=(x_{1},\dots,x_{t})$, the conditional probability of $A$ can be rewritten as

$$
\begin{aligned}
P(\theta=A\mid  \mathbf{x}_{t}) & = \left( 1 + \frac{1-\pi}{\pi}\left( \prod_{i=1}^{t} \frac{f_{B}(\mathbf{x}_{i})}{f_{A}(\mathbf{x}_{i})} \right) \right)^{-1} \\
 & = \left( 1+ \frac{1-\pi}{\pi}\left( \prod_{i=1}^{t} \frac{f_{B}(\mathbf{x}_{i}) / f_{\theta ^{\ast}}(\mathbf{x}_{i})}{f_{A}(\mathbf{x}_{i}) / f_{\theta ^{\ast}}(\mathbf{x}_{i})} \right) \right)^{-1} \\
 & = \left( 1+ \frac{1-\pi}{\pi}\exp\left( -\log\left( \prod_{i=1}^{t} R_{f_{B}} \right) + \log\left( \prod_{i=1}^{t}R_{f_{A}} \right) \right) \right) ^{-1} \\
 & = \left( 1+ \frac{1-\pi}{\pi}\exp\left( -n\cdot \left( \frac{1}{t}\sum_{i=1}^{t} \log R_{f_{B}}  - \frac{1}{t}\sum_{i=1}^{t} \log R_{f_{A}}\right) \right) \right)^{-1}
\end{aligned}
$$

where $R_{f_{\theta}}= f_{\theta ^{\ast}}(\mathbf{x}_{i}) / f_{\theta}(\mathbf{x}_{i})$ for $\theta \in \{ A,B \}$.

For large $t$, the limiting belief is approximately determined by,

$$
\left( 1 + \frac{1-\pi}{\pi}\exp(-t\cdot(D_{KL}(f_{\theta ^{\ast}}\mid  \mid  f_{B}) - D_{KL}(f_{\theta ^{\ast}}\mid  \mid  f_{A}))) \right)^{-1} \tag{$\dagger$}
$$

If $\theta ^{\ast}\in \{ A,B \}$, then either $D(f_{\theta ^{\ast}}\mid \mid f_{A})=0<D(f_{\theta ^{\ast}}\mid |f_{B})$ (in which case the limiting belief converges to $1$) or $D(f_{\theta ^{\ast}}\mid \mid f_{B})=0<D(f_{\theta ^{\ast}}\mid \mid f_{A})$ (in which case the limiting belief converges to zero). In either case, beliefs converge to certainty of the true parameter as implied by [[Doob's Consistency Theorem]].

Suppose now that $\theta ^{\ast}\not\in \{ A,B \}$. Since [[Doob's Consistency Theorem#^ml-alias-doob-inconsistent-measure-zero]] , the theorem no longer applies. $\theta ^{\ast}$ falls in the $P-$measure zero set on which consistency is not guaranteed.

However, $(\dagger)$ is still useful even when $\theta ^{\ast}$ has zero probability under the agent's prior. See [[Berk's Consistency Result]] for a discussion.
