>[!property] Property (Maximal Value).
>$$
>H(p)\leq H\left(  \frac{1}{n},\dots,\ \frac{1}{n} \right)\qquad \forall n<\infty \text{ and }p \in\Delta(\{ \theta_{1},\dots,\theta_{n} \})
>$$
 that is, entropy is maximized at the [[Uniform Distribution]].
 
The property of [[Shannon Entropy|entropy]] captures the idea that the "most random" distribution is the _most uncertain_. In fact, the term "maximal value" is justified by providing the basis for the bound of total entropy for a given distribution, as in [[= Gibbs' Inequality#^9cc265|the corollary to Gibbs' Inequality]].
