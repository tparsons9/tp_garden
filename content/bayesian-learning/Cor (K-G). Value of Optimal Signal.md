The signal $\sigma$ is a mechanism that generates a distribution of posterior beliefs $\tau \in\Delta(\Delta(\Theta))$. By [[Beliefs are a Martingale]] and [[Bayes' Plausible#^signal-induced-dist-iff-bayes-plausible]], for $\tau$ to be generated by some signal $\sigma$, it must be **Bayes' Plausible**. That is,

$$
\int_{\Delta(\Theta)}\mu\,d\tau(\mu)=\mu_{0}
$$

that is, averaged over distribution $\tau$, [[Bayes' Plausible|the expected posterior is equal to the prior]].

By [[Value of a Signal (Kamenica-Gentzkow)]], the optimal signal values $v^{\ast}$ is achieved *if and only if* $\mathbb{E}_{\tau}[\hat{v}(\mu)]=v^{\ast}$.

Therefore, the Sender's problem becomes an optimization problem over the set of Bayes-plausible distributions $\tau$.

> [!corollary] Corollary (Value of an optimal signal).
> The value of an optimal signal is
> $$
> \max_{\tau \in\Delta(\Delta(\Theta))}\mathbb{E}_{\tau}[\hat{v}(\mu)]\quad\text{s.t.}\quad \int_{\Delta(\Theta)}\mu\,d\tau(\mu)=\mu_{0} 
>$$
> 

^ml-cor-bayes-persuasion-value-of-optimal-signal


The maximization seeks the highest expected value of $\hat{v}(\mu)$, achieved by splitting the prior $\mu_{0}$ into different posterior beliefs $(\mu_{i})$ weighted by probabilities $(\alpha_{i})$ such that $\sum \alpha_{i}\mu_{i}=\mu_{0}$. Note that this implies that the optimal signal is in the convex hull $\text{conv}(\hat{v})$, i.e., [[Bayesian Persuasion - Solution-concept as a Upper Concave Envelope#^ml-def-concave-envelope-sender-indirect-utility]] .
